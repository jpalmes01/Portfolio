Greyhounds Oasis

A tail of how thoughtful design can bridge the gap between users and a noble mission: one pixel at a time.

The Problem

The current website had many issues such as faulty interfaces and outdated information, leaving potential adopters feeling frustrated and unsure about the adoption process. These issues can lead to lower adoption rates, and higher return rates.

The Solution

Our goal was to create a user-friendly, visually appealing, and informative website that effectively communicates the organization's mission, facilitates seamless adoption processes, and serves as a comprehensive resource hub for greyhound enthusiasts.

Impact

Our redesign has the potential to result in higher adoption rates, fewer pets being returned, better care for animals, an enhanced organization image, and a tighter-knit community of greyhound enthusiasts.

Racing Home

For years, greyhounds have graced racetracks with their remarkable speed and agility. Yet, the end of their racing careers often marks the beginning of an uncertain future. The greyhound racing industry produces a surplus of greyhounds every year, and those that are not rescued are unfortunately killed. Rescue organizations such a Greyhounds Only, Inc. are dedicated to finding retired greyhounds their forever homes, where they can finally experience the simple joys of leisurely walks, soft beds, and enjoy the retirement they deserve. United by a shared passion for rescue animals, every member of our team has a furry friend from a shelter. This personal connection fueled our inspiration to revamp the Greyhounds Only site because we deeply understand and value the impact of these second chances.

User Research

To truly grasp user preferences and pinpoint the problems with the existing website, we employed a combination of techniques. First, we reached out to the Greyhounds Only organization to see if we could interview them to get their perspective. Unfortunately we did not hear back and had to go on without a stakeholder interview. We then conducted five user interviews and usability tests on the current website to identify pain points, create a user persona and chart a user path.


Findings from user interviews exposed multiple issues:


All these pain points made the users feel distant and doubtful about what's on the website. As a result, there's a gap between users and the mission of the website.
We conducted a UX survey about animal rescue organizations and also polled people on social media regarding their thoughts on changing to a more inclusive name. Transitioning from "Greyhounds Only" to "Greyhounds Oasis" suggests a shift from exclusivity to a more open and inviting space. The word "oasis" adds a touch of warmth and comfort, a welcoming resting spot for greyhounds.

91% of responses were in favor of the name change.
